Nursing Program — Key Updates & Action Items
Curriculum Review
Spring 2026: faculty began a thorough review of the AS Nursing curriculum at both the program and course level. Improvements based on faculty expertise and MOHHS nurse input are under review by the curriculum committee.
New Faculty Hired
Fall 2025: CMI hired a new nursing faculty member with a PhD in Hospital Administration, MBA in Health Care Services, and MSc in Nursing — a key step toward programmatic accreditation.
International Students
Fall 2025 enrolled nursing majors included 9 students from the FSM and 2 from the Philippines — growing the international pipeline into RMI healthcare.
Accreditation Pathway
CMI expects to submit its initial application to ASEN (programmatic accreditor for associate-level nursing programs) by April 2026, beginning the candidacy process toward full accreditation.
CNA & LPN Programs
CMI met with MOHHS stakeholders on the RMI nursing shortage. Plans developed for a Certified Nursing Assistant (CNA) program in high schools and a Licensed Practical Nurse (LPN) certificate — both as stepping stones to the ASN degree.
CHOW Completion
CHOW students completing their program in Spring 2026 will enroll in the Certificate of Completion in Outer Atoll Health Assistant (CC-OAHA).

About ACCJC
The Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges (ACCJC) is CMI's regional accreditor. ACCJC accreditation affirms that CMI meets established standards of quality and is authorized to award degrees and certificates recognized by U.S. federal and state agencies.
Why it matters: Accreditation is required for students to access federal financial aid, for CMI's degrees to be recognized by employers and transfer institutions, and for the college to remain eligible for federal grant programs including Title IV.
📌

Warning vs. Probation

Warning is the first level of sanction. If CMI fails to meet the November 1 deadline, ACCJC could escalate to Probation or Show Cause — a serious outcome that could affect student aid eligibility.

✅

Midterm Accepted

ACCJC accepted CMI's Midterm Report in June 2025 — confirming the college has made meaningful progress on its core standards since the last comprehensive review.

ISER Committees — Structure & Standards Coverage
Following the ACCJC liaison visit in April 2025, CMI formed four committees to develop the Institutional Self Evaluation Report. Each committee is co-chaired by a faculty and a staff member and is responsible for gathering documentation and evidence for their respective standards area.
Standard I
Institutional Mission & Effectiveness

Covers CMI's mission statement, institutional planning, program review processes, and how the college evaluates its own effectiveness in achieving mission-driven outcomes.

Standard II
Student Success

Covers instructional programs, student support services, library and learning resources, and how CMI ensures students achieve stated learning outcomes and completion goals.

Standard III
Infrastructure & Resources

Covers human resources, physical and technology infrastructure, financial resources, and the college's capacity to sustain and improve operations in support of its mission.

Standard IV
Governance & Decision-Making

Covers board governance, administrative leadership, faculty and staff participation in shared governance, and how CMI makes decisions that reflect institutional integrity and accountability.
